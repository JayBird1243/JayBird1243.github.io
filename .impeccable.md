## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are recruiters and engineering managers evaluating technical depth, communication clarity, and project ownership. They are typically scanning quickly first, then drilling deeper into selected work. Their core job is to understand what Jack has built, how complex the work is, and whether his skills map to open roles.

### Brand Personality
The brand voice is curious, thorough, and unique. The interface should feel like an engineer-designer who explores deeply, explains clearly, and presents work with personality instead of generic portfolio tropes. Emotional target is wonder: visitors should feel intrigued and pulled into exploring projects in more detail.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first visual system with procedural gradients and a star-field aesthetic as a signature motif. The look should remain technical and immersive while preserving readability and scannability for hiring decision-makers. Visual differentiation comes from deterministic generative backgrounds and polished micro-interactions that reinforce project depth.

### Design Principles
1. Optimize for recruiter scan speed first, depth second.
2. Make project outcomes and technical substance immediately legible.
3. Preserve the dark procedural-star identity as a distinctive brand anchor.
4. Evoke wonder through purposeful motion and generative detail, not clutter.
5. Keep information architecture comprehensive but easy to parse under time pressure.
